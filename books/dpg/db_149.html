11.3.4 Polymorphism

An important advantage of programming in Dylan is that we can provide a general method for copying a sequence (as shown in Section 11.3.2), and also can provide special copying methods for particular subclasses of sequences (as shown in Section 11.3.3). Method dispatch takes care of picking the best method for the argument. Callers of my-copy-sequence do not need to worry about any performance optimizations that we have installed for lists. They simply use my-copy-sequence for lists, just as they would for any other sequence. This polymorphism can be useful for keeping interfaces between components of a program simple and extensible.
